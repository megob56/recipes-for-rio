# Enchiladas

## Ingredients

### Base
<ul>
  <li>4 tortillas</li>
</ul>

### Filling
<ul>
  <li>1 bell pepper</li>
  <li>1 zuchinni</li>
  <li>1 can black beans</li>
  <li>Cooked chicken breast (or substitute butternut squash for all veggie enchiladas)</li>
</ul>

### Sauce
<ul>
  <li>3 tablespoons olive oil</li>
  <li>3 tablespoons flour</li>
  <li>2 tablespoons tomato paste</li>
  <li>1 tablespoon chili powder (less if it's the hot, not mild, chili powder)</li>
  <li>2 1/2 cups water</li>
  <li>1 chicken or vegetable stock cube</li>
  <li>1 teaspoon cumin</li>
  <li>1/4 teaspoon oregano</li>
  <li>salt</li>
  <li>pepper</li>
</ul>

### Optional toppings
<ul>
  <li>Cheddar cheese</li>
  <li>Chopped cilantro</li>
</ul>

## Method

### For the filling
<ol type="1">
  <li>Preheat the oven to 180 C (350 F)</li>
  <li>Chop the bell pepper and zuchinni in to chunks (and the squash as well if using)</li>
  <li>In a medium sized pan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat</li>
  <li>Add the veggies to the pan and cook until soft (about 15-20 minutes)</li>
  <li>Add the can of black beans to the same pot, when there is about 5-8 minutes left for the veggies to cook</li>
  <li>While the veggies are cooking, prepare the sauce in a small sauce pan</li>
</ol> 

### For the sauce
<ol type="1">
  <li>While the veggies are cooking, prepare the sauce in a small sauce pan</li>
  <li>Turn the heat on VERY low</li>
  <li>Add the olive oil, flour, chili poweder and tomato paste and stir together in the sauce pan until a thick paste forms</li>
  <li>Add the water and turn up the heat to medium/high</li>
  <li>Add the stock cube</li>
  <li>Add the cumin, oregano, salt & pepper to taste</li>
  <li>Mix ingredients thoroughly, making sure nothing sticks to the bottom. May be helpful to use a whisk to make sure all the ingredients are incorporated</li>
  <li>Bring the sauce to a simmer for 15-20 minutes until it is reduced slightly and no longer very watery</li>
</ol> 

### Bring it all together
<ol type='1'>
  <li>In a 9 x13 inch baking pan, lay out 4 tortillas</li>
  <li>Fill each tortilla with the filling (~ 3-4 tablespoons of filling)</li>
  <li>Roll the filled tortilla up</li>
  <li>Once all tortillas are assembled in the pan, pour the sauce over the top of the tortillas</li>
  <li>Sprinkle the cheddar cheese on top of the saucy tortillas</li>
  <li>Bake in the oven for approximately 10 minutes, or until cheese is melted</li>
  <li>Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro</li>
</ol>
